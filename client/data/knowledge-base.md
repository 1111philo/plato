# AI Leaders — Knowledge Base & FAQ

**Purpose:** This document serves as a comprehensive knowledge base for understanding the AI Leaders program — what it is, how it works, who it's for, and how its learning platform is built on core learning sciences. Load this as context for any agent or system that needs to understand AI Leaders.

## 1. What Is AI Leaders?

AI Leaders is a workforce development and AI literacy program operated by 11:11 Philosophers (also referred to as "11:11" or "1111 Philo"). The program brings together students and professionals from partner universities — primarily the University of Illinois Chicago (UIC), Louisiana Tech University, and the University of Louisiana at Lafayette (ULL) — to build practical AI skills through hands-on, portfolio-driven coursework.

The program's core philosophy is "learn by doing." Participants don't just study AI concepts in the abstract — they build real things (starting with WordPress sites and AI-augmented workflows) and assemble a professional portfolio that demonstrates their capabilities to employers.

11:11's mission is to bring together technology and community to achieve measurable public benefit.

## 2. Program Structure & Timeline

### Cohort Overview (Beta / Pilot Cohort — Spring 2026)

- **Orientation:** Two synchronous Zoom sessions held the week of March 17–19, 2026, plus an asynchronous option (slideshow + recorded video) for those who couldn't attend live.

## 3. Frequently Asked Questions

### Program Basics

**Q: What is the AI Leaders program?**
A: AI Leaders is a free, portfolio-driven AI literacy and workforce development program. Participants learn to use AI tools (starting with Claude and WordPress) through hands-on coursework, building a real professional portfolio along the way.

**Q: Who can participate?**
A: The current cohort draws primarily from students at UIC, Louisiana Tech, and ULL. The program was also opened to community members in the New Orleans and Chicago areas. You must have been accepted to participate.

**Q: Does the program cost anything?**
A: No. The program is completely free for participants.

**Q: Is there a stipend?**
A: A $1,000 completion award is available for the 40 participants selected for the job placement track. Selection is based on portfolio quality, evaluated against a rubric that will be released in April.

**Q: Where does the course take place?**
A: Slack is the communication hub. Course materials and portfolio-building activities happen on the 1111 Learn platform (a custom GenAI-powered web application). There is no separate LMS to join.

**Q: When does the course start?**
A: The first course launches on April 1, 2026. Use the time before launch to get to know other participants in Slack and polish your online profiles.

### Orientation & Getting Started

**Q: I missed the live orientation. How do I catch up?**
A: Watch the orientation recording on YouTube while reviewing the slideshow. Then confirm completion by replying to the pinned orientation thread in `#ai-leaders-participants`.

**Q: Do I need to use my school email?**
A: No. You can use your personal email. You'll be able to change your email when you get access to the learning platform.

**Q: What should I do before the course starts?**
A: Polish your online profiles (LinkedIn, Bluesky, X, etc.). The first step of the AI Leaders course involves sharing your online profile. You can also explore wordpress.org and start getting familiar with AI tools like Claude.

### Portfolio, Certification & Job Placement

**Q: What is the portfolio?**
A: The portfolio is the central deliverable of the program. As you work through courses, every activity contributes to building a real, professional portfolio that showcases your AI skills and work. The hope is that it becomes an authentic tool you use in your real career.

**Q: Should I pick a focus topic for my portfolio?**
A: Yes — build your portfolio around topics you're genuinely passionate about. Whether that's healthcare, education, cybersecurity, marketing, creative technology, or something else entirely, the portfolio should be authentic to your interests and goals.

**Q: Is certification available to everyone or just the 40 selected participants?**
A: Certification is available to all participants who complete the course. However, the most impactful thing for employment will be the portfolio itself — the work you can demonstrate matters more than the credential alone.

**Q: How are the 40 job placement participants selected?**
A: Selection is based on portfolio quality. A detailed rubric will be released in April 2026. You must be a student at one of the three partner universities (UIC, Louisiana Tech, or ULL) to be eligible for the placement track.

**Q: What kind of job opportunities are available?**
A: Participants learn about specific opportunities as part of the coursework, so they can decide which align with their interests and skills. Partner companies and roles will be introduced during the program.

**Q: If I'm not selected for the 40 placements, can I still benefit?**
A: Absolutely. Everyone has access to course materials and builds a portfolio. The skills, portfolio, and certification you earn are valuable regardless of placement selection. Future cohorts may also offer additional opportunities.

**Q: Who owns the work I create?**
A: You own your intellectual property. The program's privacy policy is published at github.com/1111philo/learn/blob/main/PRIVACY.md.

### Support & Community

**Q: Are we assigned mentors?**
A: There are no assigned mentors. For personal support, DM Blake, Stefin, or Asya directly. General questions should be posted in `#ai-leaders-participants` so the community can help.

**Q: Can I use AI tools like Claude or Lovable to build my portfolio?**
A: Yes — using AI tools is encouraged and is core to what the program teaches.

**Q: Is this a pilot / beta program?**
A: Yes. This is the first cohort. Participant feedback will shape both the current experience and future versions of the program.

**Q: Can I contribute to the platform itself?**
A: Yes. Technically-inclined participants can contribute via GitHub (e.g., PRs to the Slack service or the learning platform extension). QA testers are also welcomed.

## 4. Who Are the Participants?

The cohort is remarkably diverse — spanning disciplines, career stages, and geographies. Common backgrounds include:

- Computer Science & Engineering students (UIC, Louisiana Tech, ULL)
- Healthcare professionals and students (nursing, health informatics, healthcare administration, OB/GYN sonography)
- Education professionals (K-12 STEM teachers, instructional designers, English instructors, doctoral students in education)
- Business and marketing students (finance, marketing analytics, data-driven marketing)
- Creative professionals (creative directors, freelance developers, musicians, artists)
- Law and policy students (pre-law, public policy, paralegal work)
- Cybersecurity and IT professionals
- International participants (e.g., tech workers from Brazil, Nicaragua)

### Common Interests Across the Cohort

- AI as augmentation (not automation) — using AI to enhance human capabilities rather than replace them
- AI ethics, privacy, and accessibility
- AI in healthcare (EHR documentation, patient outcomes, cost-effective care)
- AI in education (classroom chatbots, learning tools, pedagogy)
- WordPress and AI-assisted web development
- Building with AI tools (Claude, Lovable, Midjourney, etc.)
- Entrepreneurship — using AI to start or grow businesses

## 6. Key Values & Culture

- **"Learn by doing"** — The foundational philosophy. The best way to learn AI is to use it.
- **Community-driven** — Participants are encouraged to answer each other's questions, create their own Slack channels, organize meetups, and contribute to the platform.
- **AI as augmentation** — The program positions AI as a tool that enhances human capabilities, not one that replaces human work.
- **Portfolio over credential** — The most impactful outcome is the work you can demonstrate, not the certificate itself.
- **Feedback shapes the program** — As a pilot cohort, participant feedback directly influences both the current experience and future iterations.
